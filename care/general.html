EMERGENCY CARE


Signs Your Pet Needs Emergency Care

• Pale gums • Rapid breathing • Weak/rapid pulse

• Change in body temperature • Difficulty standing • Paralysis

• Loss of consciousness • Seizures • Excessive bleeding

First Aid

  1. External bleeding due to trauma:
    elevate wound and apply pressure to it
  2. Choking:
    Place fingers into mouth to check if blockage can be removed
  3. Choking (unable to remove by hand):
    Perform a modified Heimlich maneuver by giving a sharp rap to the chest

Daily needs

  1. Excercise
    (throw ball, run)
  2. Brush coat
    (only bath a few times in a year)

Pay attention to their health

  • See vet once a year
  • Flea comb to find and remove fleas
  • Female dogs should be spayed by 6 months
  • Male dogs neutered by 6 months

CPR

  1. Place on side
  2. Perform artificial respiration:
    Extending the head and neck while holding the jaws closed and blowing into the nostrils once every three seconds
    (Ensure no air escapes between your mouth and the pet’s nose.)
  3. No heartbeat:
    3 chest compressions for every respiration, until breathing resumes

Poison

Call veterinarian
